Competences of the Graduates
The main focus is on computing and networking technologies. The program is designed to offer both a broad-based education in technologies and a general multidisciplinary education.
In addition to the core courses in computers, programming, networking technologies, and related project work, students study math, physics and communications. Together these courses will provide students with a solid and well-rounded framework on which to build a career.

Requirements for Admission
NBU Entry Test, Certificate for English language level (minimum 500 points TOEFL or other equivalent) or English entry exam

Requirements for Graduates: Total number of credits 256 and 8 training courses - 60 credits per year, as written below:

Training courses – 2

Fourth year:
Programme courses under the module chosen – 60 cr.

Free Bachelor thesis – 10 cr.

Total number of hours: 2640

Possibilities for job
Graduates could be doing work in any of the following areas: Building network infrastructure and internet working Architecture design and usability testing Software development for businesses Database and web application development. Students could be working for large telecom companies, software development companies, any company or organization requiring network support, design and management. And they could be doing this work in government, business or the non-profit sector - in virtually any sector of the economy.
